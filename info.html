Two Factor Authentication

Two factor authentication is a security measure in which the user needs to provide two means of identification, one of which is typically a knowledge factor, such as some information to be memorized and other one is a possession factor such as a card.

A common example of two-factor authentication is an ATM card: the iteself is the physical item and the personal identification number (PIN) is the data that goes with it.
